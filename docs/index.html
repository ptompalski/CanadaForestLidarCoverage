ALS data coverage in Canadian forests

Last modified

November 2, 2025

This webpage is designed to present the current status of airborne laser scanning (ALS) data coverage across Canada’s forested landscapes. We present a collection of maps and descriptive statistics that illustrate the current status and availability of ALS data, specifically focusing on its applications within forestry. ALS acquisitions outside Canada’s forested ecozones (e.g. in urban areas) or data acquired for research projects are not included.

This webpage accompanies the publication by White et al. 2025, and is designed to keep the statistics and maps presented in the paper up to date.

White, J.C., Tompalski, P., Bater, C.W., Wulder, M.A., Fortin, M., Hennigar, C., Robere-McGugan, G., Sinclair, I., White, R. (2025). Enhanced forest inventories in Canada: implementation, status, and research needs. Canadian Journal of Forest Research, 55: 1–37. doi.org/10.1139/cjfr-2024-0255.

Information on this website is periodically updated to ensure the most current and accurate representation of ALS data coverage. See ALS coverage data sources to find the date the coverage was updated for each jurisdiction.

Most recent coverage summary

Total ALS Coverage

1.98M km²

21.7% of total land area of Canada.

Coverage of forested ecozones

30.3%

Coverage of managed forests

71.9%

The total area of ALS coverage in Canada is currently 1,977,547 km2, which corresponds to approximately 21.7% of the total land area of the country. The coverage of all forested ecozones is approximately 30.3%, while the coverage of managed forests is approximately 71.9%.
