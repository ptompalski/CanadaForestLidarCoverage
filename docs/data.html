ALS coverage data sources

The data used in this compilation was obtained from a number of sources including online services and by directly contacting Provincial and Territorial departments responsible for data management. Because some of the coverage data are not available for the public, the vector layers presented on this website cannot be shared.

The table below lists the sources of coverage data by jurisdiction. Note that “Last update” corresponds to the date the coverage map for each jurisdiction was updated, and is not indicative of the most recent ALS acquisition.

Jurisdiction Last update Data source

ALS data availability

Jurisdiction Data sharing policy
BC ALS - open data.
DEM and CHM from systematic provincial acquisition will be shared.
www.lidar.gov.bc.ca
AB GoA: Initial provincial coverage ALS data and derivatives were acquired with a licence (i.e., data were leased for 99 years). Sharing is limited to GoA partners (e.g. academia) for collaborative projects and forest industry.
Industry: 11 projects between 2017 and 2022 funded through the Forest Resource Improvement Program (FRIP). Data are not currently open, although FRIP is public funds.
ABMI acquisitions will be made freely available.
SK ALS point clouds currently not shared as open data.
Derivatives (DEM) are shared openly (not all derived from lidar): environment-saskatchewan.hub.arcgis.com
ON ALS point clouds and derivatives (DEM, CHM, DSM) are open data, available at geohub.lio.gov.on.ca
QC ALS data available for purchase (geoboutique.mern.gouv.qc.ca).
Derivatives (DEM, CHM, hillshade, slope) available as open data: https://www.donneesquebec.ca/
NB ALS data are open and available for download: www.snb.ca/geonb1/e/dc/lidar.asp.
Derivatives (i.e. DEM, CHM, hillshade, slope) are open and are available as WMS/WCS service.
PE ALS data available for download: download-telecharger.services.geo.ca
NS ALS files and derivatives are open data (nsgi.novascotia.ca/datalocator/elevation/).

Additional ALS datasets in Canada

ALS data acquired outside of forest ecosystems (e.g., in urban areas or for flood mapping purposes) or for research purposes are not included in the maps and summaries above. Some of those datasets include:

  • High Resolution Digital Elevation Model (HRDEM) - CanElevation Series

  • High Resolution Digital Elevation Model Mosaic (HRDEM Mosaic) CanElevation Series

  • National Elevation Data Strategy

  • LiDAR Point Clouds - CanElevation Series

  • Petawawa Research Forest
